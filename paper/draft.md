# Does Benchmark Choice Change Measured Factor Premia in India?

## Abstract

This paper examines whether measured equity factor premia in India are sensitive to benchmark and investment-universe choice. Using public Indian equity data, the study compares size, value, momentum, and low-volatility factor returns across benchmark universes such as Nifty 50, Nifty 100, Nifty 500, and a broader liquid NSE universe. The paper contributes by showing whether factor magnitude, statistical significance, drawdowns, and investability depend on the benchmark universe used by researchers and practitioners.

## 1. Introduction

Indian equity factor research often reports factor premia without fully separating the role of benchmark selection. A factor measured inside a concentrated large-cap universe may differ materially from the same factor measured across a broader equity universe. This study asks whether benchmark choice changes the observed factor evidence in India.

## 2. Research Question

Does benchmark or investment-universe choice change measured Indian equity factor premia?

## 3. Hypotheses

### H1: Factor premia differ across benchmark universes.

The magnitude and statistical significance of factor premia vary across Nifty 50, Nifty 100, Nifty 500, and a broader liquid NSE universe.

### H2: The size factor is underestimated in narrow large-cap universes.

A concentrated large-cap universe cannot fully capture the small-minus-big effect.

### H3: Value and momentum are more sensitive to universe definition than low volatility.

Value and momentum may depend more strongly on the inclusion of mid-cap and smaller securities.

### H4: Liquidity filters materially affect measured factor premia.

A factor that appears strong before liquidity filtering may weaken after excluding illiquid stocks.

## 4. Data

The study is designed to rely only on freely accessible public data. Potential sources include NSE historical price-volume archives, NSE bhavcopies, NSE Indices factsheets, public financial statements, and RBI risk-free-rate data.

## 5. Methodology

The study constructs factor portfolios separately within each benchmark universe. Securities are sorted into portfolios based on factor signals, and long-short factor returns are computed. Results are compared across universes using return statistics, t-statistics, drawdowns, volatility, Sharpe ratios, and robustness checks.

## 6. Expected Contribution

The paper contributes to Indian factor-investing research by demonstrating whether factor evidence is partly a benchmark-construction effect. The reproducible code and public-data workflow are intended to support future research on Indian equity factor premia.

## 7. Limitations

The main methodological risks are survivorship bias, limited historical constituent availability, corporate-action adjustments, ticker changes, and liquidity distortions in smaller stocks.
